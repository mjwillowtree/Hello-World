# Post-2025 529->ABLE Rollover Status Memo (Advisor-Safe)

Updated: 2026-03-10

## Question

Did the federal rule allowing rollovers from 529 plans to ABLE accounts expire after 2025, or is it now permanent?

## Short answer

The sunset was removed. Federal law now supports 529->ABLE rollovers after 2025, subject to the existing statutory conditions (beneficiary/family-member rule, 60-day rollover timing, and ABLE annual contribution cap interaction).

## Tier 1 controlling authority

1. Public Law amendment text (controlling):

   - Pub. L. 119-21, Sec. 70117(a):
     - "Section 529(c)(3)(C)(i)(III) is amended by striking 'before January 1, 2026,'."
   - Pub. L. 119-21, Sec. 70117(b):
     - "The amendment made by this section shall apply to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2025."
   - Source: https://www.congress.gov/119/plaws/publ21/PLAW-119publ21.pdf

2. Current codified IRC text (reflects amendment):

   - IRC 529(c)(3)(C)(i)(III):
     - "(III) to an ABLE account (as defined in section 529A(e)(6)) of the designated beneficiary or a member of the family of the designated beneficiary."
   - Same subparagraph reminder:
     - "Subclause (III) shall not apply to so much of a distribution which, when added to all other contributions made to the ABLE account for the taxable year, exceeds the limitation under section 529A(b)(2)(B)(i)."
   - Source: https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title26-section529&num=0&edition=prelim

3. Codification note confirming the exact removal:

   - U.S. Code amendment note (2025):
     - "Subsec. (c)(3)(C)(i)(III). Pub. L. 119-21, Sec. 70117(a), struck out 'before January 1, 2026,' before 'to an ABLE account'."
   - Source: https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title26-section529&num=0&edition=prelim

## Practical interpretation

- For post-2025 tax years, federal rollover authority under IRC 529(c)(3)(C)(i)(III) no longer carries a built-in expiration date.
- Planners should still verify:
  - rollover recipient eligibility (same beneficiary or qualifying family member),
  - 60-day transfer mechanics under IRC 529 rollover framework,
  - annual ABLE contribution limit coordination (including ABLE-to-Work when applicable),
  - state tax conformity and recapture treatment.

## Authority hierarchy for conflict resolution

When sources disagree, use this order:

1. Statute (IRC text + enacted public law),
2. Regulations and official administrative guidance,
3. Publications/newsroom pages and plan marketing content.

If a lower-tier source still implies pre-2026 expiration, treat it as stale and anchor advice to the amended statute.

## Residual caveats

- Federal legality is now high confidence.
- State recapture and filing mechanics remain jurisdiction-specific and can still create tax friction even when federal treatment is qualified.
