# Post-2025 529->ABLE Rollover Status Memo

Updated: 2026-03-10

## Question

Are tax-free rollovers from a section 529 plan to an ABLE account still available for tax years after 2025?

## Short answer (advisor-safe)

Yes. Current controlling law removes the prior "before January 1, 2026" cutoff, so 529->ABLE rollover treatment continues for years after 2025 (subject to all other statutory conditions and limits).

## Controlling authority (Tier 1)

1. **Public Law amendment to IRC 529**
   - Pub. L. 119-21, **Sec. 70117(a)**: amends IRC 529(c)(3)(C)(i)(III) by striking "before January 1, 2026,".
   - Effective date in **Sec. 70117(b)**: applies to taxable years beginning after Dec. 31, 2025.
   - Source: https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/PLAW-119publ21/html/PLAW-119publ21.htm

2. **Public Law amendment to IRC 529A annual-limit interaction**
   - Pub. L. 119-21, **Sec. 70115(a)(2)**: strikes "before January 1, 2026" in IRC 529A(b)(2)(B)(ii).
   - Effective date in **Sec. 70115(b)**: applies to contributions made after Dec. 31, 2025 (with separate timing for indexed amount mechanics).
   - Source: https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/PLAW-119publ21/html/PLAW-119publ21.htm

3. **Current U.S. Code editorial text**
   - IRC 529(c)(3)(C)(i)(III) now shows ABLE rollover clause without the date restriction.
   - Code notes explicitly state the 2025 amendment struck the "before January 1, 2026" text.
   - Source: https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?edition=prelim&req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title26-section529

4. **Related 529A note**
   - U.S. Code notes for IRC 529A reflect the same deletion in 529A(b)(2)(B)(ii).
   - Source: https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?edition=prelim&req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title26-section529A

## Implementation artifacts (lower authority than statute)

- 2025 IRS Instructions for Forms 1099-QA/5498-QA still treat section 529-to-ABLE rollovers as contribution-category amounts and discuss annual limit interaction, without a "before 2026" limiter in the rollover references reviewed.
  - Source: https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i1099qa

- Legacy IRS newsroom pages remain useful background but are not controlling and may contain outdated examples/thresholds.
  - Example legacy article: https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/tax-reform-affects-able-accounts-savers-credit-529-rollovers

## Practical interpretation rule

When authority conflicts or appears stale, use:
1. Statute/public law text,
2. Final regulations,
3. IRS forms/instructions,
4. Publications/newsroom pages,
5. Plan marketing/FAQ pages.

## Conservative client-language fallback

"Under current federal statutory text, the prior 2025 sunset language for 529-to-ABLE rollovers was removed for post-2025 years. We still validate annual contribution-cap interaction, same-beneficiary/family-member eligibility, and state tax conformity/recapture before execution."

## Residual risk flags

1. State conformity can diverge from federal treatment.
2. Program operations (coding/forms/timing) can still cause failed or mis-coded transfers.
3. IRS sub-regulatory documents can lag statutory changes; re-check each filing season.
