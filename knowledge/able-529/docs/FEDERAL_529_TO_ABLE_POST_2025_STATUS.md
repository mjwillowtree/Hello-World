# Federal Memo: 529->ABLE Rollover Status After 2025

Updated: 2026-03-10

## Bottom line (advisor-safe)

For 2026 and later, treat 529->ABLE rollovers as federally available, provided the transfer satisfies IRC §529(c)(3)(C) conditions (including the 60-day transfer rule, same-beneficiary/family-member pathway, and ABLE annual-cap coordination under §529A).

## Why this is the best current legal reading

### 1) Current operative IRC §529 text contains no 2025 sunset phrase

Current U.S. Code text for IRC §529(c)(3)(C)(i)(III) includes rollover "to an ABLE account ... of the designated beneficiary or a member of the family of the designated beneficiary" and does not include the prior "before January 1, 2026" limit in the operative sentence.

Source:
- https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title26-section529&num=0&edition=prelim

### 2) U.S. Code amendment note explicitly documents sunset removal

The same IRC §529 page editorial amendment note states:
- "2025 - Subsec. (c)(3)(C)(i)(III). Pub. L. 119-21, §70117(a), struck out 'before January 1, 2026,' before 'to an ABLE account'."

Source:
- https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title26-section529&num=0&edition=prelim

### 3) ABLE-side annual-limit mechanics still apply

IRC §529 still limits tax-free treatment to the extent the rollover does not exceed ABLE contribution constraints under §529A(b)(2)(B)(i). This preserves annual-cap coordination and prevents over-limit treatment.

Source:
- https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title26-section529&num=0&edition=prelim
- https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title26-section529A&num=0&edition=prelim

## Apparent contradiction and resolution

- Older IRS guidance (e.g., 2018 newsroom article) still uses temporary-language framing ("Before January 1, 2026...").
- Statutory text (as amended) controls over older sub-regulatory summaries.

Older guidance reference:
- https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/tax-reform-affects-able-accounts-savers-credit-529-rollovers

## Authority hierarchy for conflict handling

When sources disagree, use this order:
1. Enacted statute (U.S. Code / public law text).
2. Treasury regulations.
3. IRS forms/instructions/publications/newsroom.
4. Plan marketing and third-party summaries.

## Conservative practice note

Because IRS web pages and plan materials can lag statutory amendments, keep:
- a copy of the current IRC text used,
- the relevant amendment note (Pub. L. 119-21, §70117(a)),
- rollover transaction records showing beneficiary relationship and timing,
- ABLE contribution-cap computation for the tax year.
